Pancho’s Day Out

Idea by jygajyga

It’s a crisp, cool morning in the mountains; the skies are slightly overcast, with mouse-gray clouds covering the horizon. The sun begins to peek out from behind the treeline, illuminating Uncle Hugo’s house in hues of orange.

Orion clambers out of the passenger side of Nancy’s car, shutting the door behind him and hefting his backpack onto his shoulder. She rolls down the window as he begins to walk up the pathway to the front door.

NANCY: Have fun and be safe, Orion! Don’t forget to put your cooking ingredients in the fridge, or else they’ll thaw out! I’ll pick you up tomorrow morning, sound good?

Orion waves at Nancy, noticing her slightly-worried, slightly-excited expression.

ORION: Of course! Take care, safe travels, Nancy!

Nancy rolls up the window, and begins to back out of the driveway. Her car slowly drifts out of view down the hill, until the noise of the engine fades into the background. Orion takes a deep breath, and steps to Uncle Hugo’s front door.

Knock, knock.

The door clicks open, and Luna stands behind it, smiling wanly at Orion. She is dressed in a simple oversized gray t-shirt and black leggings, yet it still manages to make Orion miss a breath.

LUNA: Ah, Orion! Good to see you made it. How was the drive?

ORION: Not too bad–though, Nancy did most of the hard work. I mostly just slept in the passenger side and gave her directions at the beginning.

LUNA: Well, I’m glad to hear that you’re well rested for our–ahem.

Her voice gets slightly lower.

LUNA: Date…?

Orion chuckles.

ORION: Well, I wouldn’t want my Miss Hernandez having anything other than the full Orion experience.

Luna rolls her pomegranate eyes hard enough that they go almost back into her head.

LUNA: I’m flattered that you’d be so thoughtful, Mr. Richards.

There is a loud, thunderous stomping that suddenly comes from just beyond the houses’ threshold. Suddenly, the large frame of Luna’s uncle appears behind the girl, and he tosses the door open. He cracks a big grin at seeing Orion, and spreads his hands wide in a welcoming gesture.

UNCLE HUGO: Orion, cabro–er…my friend! It’s good to see you back here in the mountains, and with my Luna once again! She was talking all last night about your little date today. My niece here thinks very highly of you, you know.

Luna turns slightly red and flicks her eyes away from Orion, who grins at her for only a moment.

LUNA: Uncle Hugo, don’t stray so far from reality…

UNCLE HUGO: Ah, yes! Of course, of course, nina. Anyways, Orion, I’ve got a big favor to ask of you. Lunita, would you go get Pancho?

Luna seizes upon the opportunity to remove herself from the situation before she gets any redder than she already is.

LUNA: Of course, Uncle! I’ll be right back, Orion.

She slips back into the house. Uncle Hugo smiles as he watches her leave.

ORION: What can I do for you, Mr. Hernandez?

UNCLE HUGO: Orion, you certainly know that there are two things in this world that I value above all: my sweet niece Luna, and my precious boy Pancho. I have entrusted you with Luna, because I believe you are a great influence on her, and she seems very fond of you. It is now that I must entrust you with my second: Pancho.

ORION: Um…how so, Mr. Hernandez?

The smile vanishes from Uncle Hugo’s face, and his eyes widen slightly. He takes a step towards Orion, and places a single hand on the boy’s shoulder.

UNCLE HUGO: My mother and I are going out to town today–it’ll be a drive of a few hours, and we will likely be gone until sunset. I am entrusting you with care of my Luna and my Pancho. If anything happens to either one of them, well…

Orion gulps.

Uncle Hugo maintains dead eye contact with Orion, not blinking for even a moment. After an eternity in seconds passes, his smile returns and he lifts his hand from Orion.

UNCLE HUGO: But I know you won’t let that happen, will you?

ORION: No! No, Mr. Hernandez, of course not! I’d never let anyone–anything–at all come to harm either of them!

Uncle Hugo claps his hands together.

UNCLE HUGO: Excellent! I’m glad to hear it, Orion.

Luna comes back, holding Pancho in her arms, and from the demure look on her face, over the initial teasing of her uncle.

UNCLE HUGO: Ahaha! There’s my boy!

He scratches Pancho’s ruff, and the chicken clucks happily.

Just then, Luna’s grandmother trots up behind Uncle Hugo, and reaches up to grab him by the ear.

LUNA’S GRANDMOTHER: Hugo! We are going to be late if we continue at this pace!

She turns her stern gaze warm in an instant, spinning to face Orion and Luna.

LUNA’S GRANDMOTHER: But where are my manners–it is good to see you, mija and Orion!

LUNA: Hi, yaya!

ORION: Hello, Mrs. Hernandez.

Uncle Hugo nods anxiously, and tries to twist out of the hold on his ear.

ORION: (The Hernandez family really takes after each other, huh? Mr. Hernandez is the scariest guy in the room right up until his mom comes around.)

UNCLE HUGO: Of course, mama, I was just saying goodbye. Take care, Luna and–ow! Orion!

The two walk down the driveway to Uncle Hugo’s SUV with Luna’s grandmother still firmly grabbing Uncle Hugo’s ear. Their voices fade until they climb into the car and start it up.

LUNA’S GRANDMOTHER: Unbelievable! I thought I raised you better than this!

UNCLE HUGO: Mama, we’re an hour ahead of schedule!

Luna raises her eyebrows, still firmly clutching Pancho.

LUNA: My father tells me they were the same way growing up. Uncle Hugo was always the rebel of the family, and my father was the golden child. Anyway, should we–

She gestures to Orion’s backpack with her fingers, keeping a solid grasp on Pancho.

ORION: Oh, yeah. Good idea.

The two walk into the house, and Luna pushes the door shut with her foot behind her. Orion makes his way to the island, and drops the backpack onto the countertop. Unzipping it, he begins to pull out every ingredient that he and Nancy had purchased at the store earlier in the day.

ORION: Alright, let’s see…onions, garlic, shallots, vinegar, ground beef…

Cluck cluck!

Orion looks up at the noise, and not a moment later Pancho runs down the hallway and up the stairs, carrying with him a mysterious bag. Luna chases after him, sprinting up the stairs two at a time and hurriedly explaining to Orion.

LUNA: Pancho took my–ah, something of mine! Stick with the ingredients, I don’t want them to spoil! They’d probably never find your body if you served my grandma meat that was ever room-temperature.

ORION: (She can tell? Wait, did Luna say they’d never find my body?)

ORION: A-alright!

He resumes inventorying with a greater direness to his situation.

ORION: Garlic salt. Anise. Cilantro.

After a few minutes, Luna comes back down the stairs, and pads towards Orion at the counter. Looking up, he notices with a slight curiosity that her hands are gloveless. She stops slightly to the side of him. Their mouths say nothing, their eyes; everything. Luna inches slightly closer. Then, slightly closer. Then, slightly closer. Their shoulders brush.

ORION: (Oh shit, should I kiss her? Is she trying to–no, that’s not like Luna. But she’s right there, looking at me with those eyes.)

LUNA: I can’t find Pancho.

Orion involuntarily smashes his fist down on the counter, instantly macerating a head of garlic.

ORION: I’ve got it! You keep, uh, sorting through the ingredients.

LUNA: Okay.

Orion begrudgingly breaks the contact between his shoulder and Luna’s, and heads up the stairs to find Pancho. He mutters to himself as he walks down the still-unfamiliar hallway, peering into each room to find the missing chicken.

ORION (softly): Here, Pancho-Pancho-Pancho. Your dad’s gonna put me in a grave if I can’t find you…damn, if I was a chicken, where would I hide?

Suddenly, Orion’s ears perk up. Soft sounds can be heard from the room at the end of the hallway, and Orion tiptoes to the slightly-ajar door. It opens with a creak into a room he hasn’t seen before. Large windows dominate two of the walls, and a large, well-kept bed sits pushed against one wall. A picture of Uncle Hugo, Luna’s father, and Luna’s grandmother sits atop the nightstand.

ORION: (Probably Mr. Hernandez’s room.)

Cluck, cluck. Cluck.

With a sudden motion, Pancho darts out from under the bed, and in a flash of pure instinct Orion grabs him, scooping the chicken into a tight grasp.

PANCHO: Bawk! Bawk! Bawk.

He doesn’t struggle, and simply looks at Orion with his two beady eyes.

ORION: (Maybe I’ve been hanging around sentient animals in Eternum too much, but I swear this chicken is fucking with me.)

Careful not to lose concentration on containing Pancho, Orion walks back down to find Luna at the counter, with each non-perishable ingredient laid out nicely in front of her. She gives a small smile, which widens when she notices Pancho in Orion’s hands. The chicken, who was statue-still until then, wrenches himself from Orion’s protection and flaps to Luna, brushing his head against her leg.

LUNA: We’re good to start cooking! I’ve got the stove on, and the saucepan is coated with margarine. We should first cook up the peppers and beans, then brown the meat and add seasonings.

ORION: Great, yeah, yeah…

Luna nods, understanding his reservations. She rubs Pancho’s neck, and the chicken makes a soft cooing noise and flaps up to a barstool at the counter. He sits there, and studies Orion for a moment before closing his eyes and nestling his head at his chest.

LUNA: He just gets the zoomies whenever someone first comes into the house. Promise.

(A few hours pass.)

Orion drops a few dried chiles into the pot on the stove, and places the lid tightly back on the top of the pot in order to ensure the steam doesn’t take any of the carefully-balanced spices.

ORION: And that should just about settle our birria!

He mocks dusting off his hands, turning to look at Luna. She gazes up at him with big eyes, a whisper of unformed words playing at her lips.

LUNA: We’ve got a bit until it’s fully simmered. Do you want to sit on the couch?

ORION: (FUCK yes.)

ORION: Yes.

They sit on the couch, and Orion leans back into the cushions. Luna awkwardly plops down next to him and looks to the side.

ORION: (She’s getting nervous again…I mean, this is one of the only times we’ve really been alone together, and she’s still probably trying to overcome her shyness.)

LUNA: Would you like to, um, watch some trashy soap operas? My grandmother has a ton, and we used to watch them together all the time when I was younger.

ORION: Sure, sounds good to me.

Luna gets up, and rummages through the drawers under the television, finally landing on a winner and sliding it into the ancient VHS player. The screen crackles to life, displaying a slightly washed-out and fuzzy scene of a busy street corner. Satisfied, Luna walks back to the couch and gathers her courage. She sits again, this time closer to Orion, and presses play on the remote.

(A few uneventful hours go by, with the TV blaring near-unintelligible Spanish)

ORION: (We haven’t moved in forever. Is she still awake?)

He steals a glance over at Luna, who still intensely stares at the soap opera, leaning forward slightly.

LUNA: (This is still the same story I’ve seen a million times before. I hope he’s interested…we haven’t moved in forever. Is he still awake?)

She meets his eyes, and sees that he’s looking at her at the same time. She darts her eyes away, but after a moment, looks back. Orion still smiles at her, the corners of his mouth wrinkling slightly.

LUNA: (Well, here goes…)

Luna looks back at the television, and slowly leans over onto Orion, resting her head on his shoulder.

ORION: (Oh god, this is happening.)

He scoots closer to her, moving his own body to lean into Luna. They stay there for a while, and the TV continues to babble about love and betrayal, family and friends, youth and age–all in Spanish, of course.

It doesn’t matter. Neither of them are listening.

(Another hour passes.)

Orion glances over at Luna, who is now fully asleep. A tiny tuft of hair falls into her face, and her breathing is slow and soft.

ORION: (Ah, she’s so adorable when she’s sleeping.)

He surveys the house around him, relishing every element of his situation. The house is filled with aromas both spicy and sweet, and the midday sun begins to wane, casting beams of light through the windows that trace around the edges of the living room.

ORION: (I’ve got a beautiful girl sleeping on me, good food cooking on the stove, and a satisfied chicken. No, life couldn’t really get better right now.)

He admires the beautiful girl. He smells the heavenly scent of stewing birria. He does not see the chicken.

ORION: (Oh shit. I can either wake up Luna, or have Pancho gain ground on me every second I can’t see him. He could be in another country by the time Luna wakes up…)

LUNA: I felt you tense up, Orion. Is something wrong?

ORION: (Thank god.)

ORION: Pancho’s gone again.

Luna quickly stands up, breaking away from Orion’s body heat in an instant.

ORION: (Damn.)

Orion stands up as well, shaking the fatigue from his muscles. Out of the corner of his eye, he sees a streak of white feathers suddenly scrape through a hedge in the yard. Without another second of hesitation, Orion sprints to the patio door and flings it open, desperate to catch another glimpse of where Pancho went off to. The hedges near the front of he house rustle, and he runs over to investigate.

Orion and Luna chase Pancho all around the yard for another while, unsuccessfully trying to work together to corner and capture the bird. At every turn, Pancho seems to elude them, darting between Orion’s legs, jumping off of Luna’s head, and squirming into small crevices or bushes.

ORION: He’s over by the driveway again!

…

LUNA: Look, by the door. Maybe I try going to the left, and you go right this time.

…

ORION: Is he on the roof?

…

Pancho finally relents, and settles back inside on the couch. Orion and Luna nervously sit beside him, balancing their desire to keep the avian put with their desire to rest after running countless laps around Uncle Hugo’s sizable house. Outside, the sun begins to melt behind the high mountains, dazzling the once-gray clouds with streaks of pink and red.

The air has gone hazy, and the scent of warm summer rain begins to crawl through the open windows, cooling the two exhausted chicken parents as they fan themselves on the couch.

ORION: Are you alright if I–huff–take my shirt off? I think–puff–this might be the most tired I’ve ever been.

Luna quickly nods.

LUNA: (Oh god, was that too enthusiastic? I don’t want him thinking I’m a perv for wanting his shirt off. I mean I do want his shirt off, but I don’t want to come off as desperate. Maybe I–)

ORION: (Damn, she’s been nodding for a while now.)

Orion slides his shirt over his head, pulling it onto his lap and reclining further. Waves of heat seem to emanate from him like radiation. He closes his eyes, and rests his head against the back of the sofa. After a while, he sits back up.

ORION: Hey, the stew is probably–

Luna has also removed her shirt, and is sitting with her feet put up on the ottoman. She wears a lacy black bra, seemingly unaware of the one undone strap draped around her upper arm. Her porcelain skin gleams with a thin coat of sweat from all the running around. She looks over at Orion, who hastily directs his eyes to meet hers.

ORION: Ahem. Done.

LUNA: Sounds good. I’ll go check on it.

She gets up, and walks to the kitchen, pulling the lid off of the pot and releasing tides of scented steam all throughout the house. Giving Orion a thumbs-up, she replaces the lid and sits back down next to him on the couch. Just then, the front door creaks open, and Uncle Hugo’s booming voice echoes off the walls.

UNCLE HUGO: We’re hoooooooo-me!

ORION: (Oh shit. Oh shit oh shit. We’re both shirtless on his couch and covered in sweat.)

The two scramble for their shirts, hastily covering themselves before attempting to act as natural as possible. Luna examines her nails while Orion whistles and looks wistfully out the window at the sunset.

LUNA’S GRANDMOTHER: My, this house smells wonderful! What are you two cooking over here?

She shuffles over to the pot and removes the lid, taking a wooden spoon and tasting the still-boiling concoction, making absolutely no reaction other than a slight hm of approval.

Uncle Hugo walks over to the couch and seats himself in between Orion and Luna, picking up Pancho and stroking his feathers lovingly.

UNCLE HUGO: Ah, and now I’m with all of my favorite people in the world. How was everything today, Pancho?

PANCHO: Bawk. Bawk cluck.

Uncle Hugo raises his eyebrows, and interrogates the chicken with an incredulous tone.

UNCLE HUGO: Orion and Luna did what? Pancho, am I hearing you correctly?

ORION: What? You can talk to the chicken?

PANCHO: Cluck-cluck bawk!

UNCLE HUGO: …

UNCLE HUGO: Of course not! Don’t be ridiculous.

ORION: (Thank the fucking stars.)

LUNA: Uncle, I think the birria was just about done. How about we all sit at the table and discuss your day out?

ORION: Yeah, that sounds like a great idea, Luna!

(The four of them sit at the table and dish out large portions of birria alongside hefty crusts of bolillo bread.)

LUNA’S GRANDMOTHER: Hm, the broth is just the right thickness in this! Are you sure you don’t have any Mexican roots, Orion?

UNCLE HUGO: Could do with some more spice, though. Other than that, I wouldn’t change a thing!

Orion barely chokes down the stew, taking heavy breaths to reduce the spiciness in his mouth while the three Hernandezes slurp away without a care in the world.

ORION: I’ll–ack–keep it in mind, Mr. Hernandez.

After dinner, Luna’s grandmother cajoles Uncle Hugo into cleaning the dishes and tidying up the table while she cuts slices of cheesecake for dessert. She sits with Orion and Luna at the counter, and happily gossips with Luna about the soap operas they watched earlier in the day while Orion interjects with an occasional “oh, really?” or “I loved that part too!”

When the cheesecake is finished, Uncle Hugo is still slaving away at the bowls and pots while Pancho keeps him company on the counter. Luna’s grandmother yawns a little too widely, and checks her watch.

LUNA’S GRANDMOTHER: Well, it’s about that time of the night for my beauty rest. Luna, don’t forget to show Orion your surprise you had planned for today. You don’t have to tell me what it is if you don’t want to, but your abuela will find out one way or another!

She winks heartily, and Luna goes red. With that, she retreats to her room at the base of the stairs, closing the door behind her and laughing to herself. Uncle Hugo doesn’t seem to notice. Outside, the earlier signs of rain take form, with miniscule water droplets beginning to patter at the house’s windows.

ORION: I hate to invite myself, but shall we?

LUNA: Mm…okay.

The two climb the stairs once again, and pad to Luna’s room in the middle of the hall. The sun now barely peeks above the horizon, casting long shadows on the floorboards. Luna’s room is simple, yet full of character, hosting several colorful abstract paintings and scores of potted plants whose bright tones clash with the simple gray bedsheets and pillowcases.

Orion sits at the foot of Luna’s bed, and the girl turns to her dresser, pulling from the top drawer the same small bag that Pancho had stolen earlier in the day. She sits next to Orion, and pulls the drawstring. Inside is a myriad of multi-colored taffies and soft candies, wrapped in thick paper.

LUNA: These are…I made these for you.

Orion is taken aback, hesitating for a second before slowly reaching inside and grabbing a pastel pink and a light green one. He hands Luna the green one, and slowly pulls the paper off of the pink one, chewing it heartily. Sweet and salty tones coarse through his mouth, making his tongue grow heavier as he works at the candy.

ORION: Theef…are…fanfastic.

Luna laughs, a light, genuine melody. She chews at her own taffy with a great effort, and places her head on Orion’s shoulder, this time with conviction and bravery. She softly whispers in his ear.

LUNA: I’m glad you like them.

She deftly unwraps another taffy with one hand, and with the other, glides her small fingers along the sheets until they reach Orion’s. She laces her fingers with his, and opens her mouth, signaling for Orion to do the same. With a great effort, he pulls his teeth apart and accepts another candy from her. She looks away at nothing in particular.

LUNA: You know, my uncle only has two guest rooms: this one, and the one my grandmother is staying in. You said you’d spend the night, didn’t you?
